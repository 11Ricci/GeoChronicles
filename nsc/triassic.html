Triassic Period (252 – 201 million years ago)

The Triassic Period witnessed the rise of reptiles, including the first dinosaurs, and the breakup of the supercontinent Pangaea. This period also saw the diversification of life forms and the emergence of early mammals. The start of this period included the largest extinction on Earth, the Permian-Triassic Extinction, or the Great Dying while at the end of this period was the start of the Triassic-Jurassic Extinction.

Life Forms during this Period
In this period, the first dinosaurs as well as the first mammals, which were small rodents, appear. During this period, life and fauna re-diversify.
Geological Features during this Period
During this period, Pangaea breaks apart, forming Laurasia and Gondwana, with the Tethys Sea separating them.
