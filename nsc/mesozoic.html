Mesozoic Era

What is the Mesozoic Era and when was it?

The Mesozoic Era is the second era of Earth's geological history, lasting from about 252 to 66 million years ago, comprising the Triassic, Jurassic and Cretaceous Periods. It was known as the "age of reptiles" or "age of dinosaurs" and it saw the dominance of dinosaurs, the appearance of birds, and early mammals. This era ended with the Cretaceous-Paleogene extinction, which wiped out the dinosaurs.

What was the state of Earth's structure during the Mesozoic Era?

During the Mesozoic, Pangaea began separating into the modern continents. The fragmentation began with continental rifting during the Late Triassic.

What were the most prominent materials during the Mesozoic Era?

Some prominent materials during the Mesozoic era includes sandstone and limestone from desert and shallow marine environments, chalk which were formed from microscopic marine organisms, oil and natural gas which formed from organic material buried during this era and fossils that are now found in sedimentary rock.
