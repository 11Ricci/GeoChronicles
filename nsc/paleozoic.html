Paleozoic Era

What is the Paleozoic Era and when was it?

The Paleozoic Era is the first of three geological eras of the Phanerozoic Eon. Beginning 538.8 million years ago, it succeeds the Neoproterozoic and ends 251.9 Ma at the start of the Mesozoic Era.

What was the state of Earth's structure during the Paleozoic Era?

During the Paleozoic Era, Earth's continents experienced significant movement and assembly. Initially, the continents were largely gathered around the South Pole in a supercontinent called Pannotia. This supercontinent broke apart during the early Cambrian, leading to the formation of a new supercontinent called Gondwana, which included most of the southern landmasses. By the end of the Paleozoic, the continents had further coalesced into the supercontinent Pangaea.

What were the most prominent materials during the Paleozoic Era?

Abundant materials during this era included limestone and shale which were formed from marine organisms, coal which was abundant in the later Paleozoic most notably in the Carboniferous period, iron and copper ores from sedimentation and volcanic activity, and fossils such as trilobites, brachiopods, and early fish that were embedded in rock layers.
