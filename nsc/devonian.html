Devonian Period (419 – 359 million years ago)

The Devonian Period is known as the "Age of Fishes" due to the diverse marine life, including a wide array of fish species. It's also a period marked by the first land plants, the emergence of forests, and the evolution of the first tetrapods (four-legged vertebrates). Nearing the end of this period, another mass extinction, the Late Devonian Extinction, occurs.

Life Forms during this Period
Oceans still freshwater and fish migrate from southern hemisphere to North America. Amphibians, evergreens, and ferns appear.
Geological Features during this Period
During this period, the world's land formed into two supercontinents: Gondwana and Euramerica. Formation of extensive river systems and deltas occurs.
